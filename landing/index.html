01 — The problem

Research lives in fragments.

A PDF here. A text-message export there. A page that 404'd last spring. The answer exists — scattered across places that don't talk to each other.

case-file.pdf 142 pages · never fully read
messages.csv 9,400 texts · who said what, when?
profile.html link rot · gone since March
web search 40 open tabs · no system
02 — How it works

Ingest. Sift. Answer.

You ask in plain language. The agent decides where to look — your library, the live web, the archive — and does it all in one pass.

  1. 01

    Ingest

    Drop in PDFs, message exports, or web pages. Everything is chunked, embedded, and made searchable by meaning — not just keywords.

  2. 02

    Sift

    Ask a question. The agent picks its own tools — vector search across your library, a live Tavily search, a Wayback snapshot — no manual switching.

  3. 03

    Answer

    It synthesizes one coherent answer and keeps the conversation open for follow-ups — every statement traceable to its source.

03 — Capabilities

One agent. Every angle.

/01

Archive retrieval
